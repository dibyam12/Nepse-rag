NEPSE / SEBON Glossary
======================

This file defines important terms for NEPSE-focused AI responses.

NEPSE
-----
Nepal Stock Exchange, the main securities exchange in Nepal where listed
shares, mutual funds, and some debt instruments are traded electronically.

SEBON
-----
Securities Board of Nepal, the regulator overseeing the securities market.
It issues directives, approves public issues, regulates brokers, and
protects investor interests.

CDSC
----
Central Depository System & Clearing Ltd. Handles clearing, settlement,
and depository (Demat) services for securities transactions in NEPSE.

NRB
---
Nepal Rastra Bank, the central bank of Nepal. It regulates the banking
system, foreign exchange, and monetary policy. Its policies directly
affect Class A, B, and C financial institutions and, indirectly, NEPSE.

Demat Account
-------------
An electronic account where securities are held in dematerialized form.
Required for trading in NEPSE. Opened with a licensed Depository
Participant (DP).

T+2 Settlement
--------------
A settlement cycle where trades executed on day T are settled two working
days later (T+2), with shares and funds exchanged via brokers and CDSC.

Board Lot
---------
The standard trading unit (number of shares) for normal market trades.
For example, 100 units for Rs 10 face value shares.

Odd-Lot
-------
A trade with quantity smaller than the defined board lot. Traded through
a separate odd-lot mechanism.

Circuit Breaker
---------------
A pre-defined limit on price or index movement that, when hit, triggers
a trading halt or closure to prevent extreme volatility.

Daily Price Band
----------------
The allowed range for a stock’s price movement during a session, usually
defined as a percentage (for example, ±15%) of the previous day’s
closing price.

Margin Trading
--------------
Buying securities partly with borrowed funds provided by brokers or
merchant bankers under SEBON and NEPSE rules.

Bonus Share
-----------
Free share issued to existing shareholders, usually expressed as a
percentage of current holdings (for example, 20% bonus).

Rights Share
------------
Share that existing shareholders have the right to purchase at a
specified ratio and price, usually at a discount, to raise new capital.

Cash Dividend
-------------
Cash distributed to shareholders out of profits, usually expressed as a
percentage of face value or as an amount per share.

Stock Split
-----------
Corporate action that increases the number of shares by reducing the
face value proportionally (for example, 1:10 split).

Face Value
----------
The nominal value of a share (for example, Rs 10 or Rs 100) stated in
company documents, used as a base for dividends and corporate actions.

RSI
---
Relative Strength Index, a momentum oscillator that measures the speed
and magnitude of recent price changes.

MACD
----
Moving Average Convergence Divergence, a trend-following momentum
indicator based on EMAs.

EMA
---
Exponential Moving Average, a moving average that gives more weight to
recent data.

Bollinger Bands
---------------
Volatility bands plotted above and below a moving average, used to
assess overbought/oversold conditions and volatility squeezes.

ATR
---
Average True Range, a volatility indicator based on the range of price
movement including gaps.

OBV
---
On-Balance Volume, a cumulative volume indicator that adds volume on
up days and subtracts it on down days.

VWAP
----
Volume Weighted Average Price, the average price of a security for a
period weighted by volume, often used as an intraday benchmark.

Beta
----
A measure of a stock’s volatility relative to the market index. Beta
greater than 1 indicates higher volatility than the index; less than 1
indicates lower volatility.

NEPSE Alpha
-----------
A third-party analytics platform providing charts, indicators, and data
visualizations for NEPSE stocks.

Mero Lagani
-----------
A financial portal offering NEPSE-related data, news, charts, and
portfolio tools.

ShareSansar
-----------
A financial news and data website focusing on NEPSE, IPOs, and company
announcements.

Fiscal Year Format (Nepal)
--------------------------
Nepal uses a Bikram Sambat (BS) fiscal year notation, for example
“2081/82”, which roughly corresponds to mid-2024 to mid-2025 in the
Gregorian calendar. Fiscal years typically run from mid-July to mid-July.

Nepali Months (BS) and Approximate Mapping
------------------------------------------
Some common Nepali months and rough Gregorian mapping:

- Shrawan: mid-July to mid-August
- Bhadra: mid-August to mid-September
- Ashwin: mid-September to mid-October
- Kartik: mid-October to mid-November
- Mangsir: mid-November to mid-December
- Poush: mid-December to mid-January
- Magh: mid-January to mid-February
- Falgun: mid-February to mid-March
- Chaitra: mid-March to mid-April
- Baishakh: mid-April to mid-May
- Jestha: mid-May to mid-June
- Ashadh: mid-June to mid-July
