SECTION A: MANUFACTURING AND PROCESSING SECTOR
=============================================================================

Sub-Index: Manufacturing And Processing
Listed Companies: 26
Sub-Index Level (May 8, 2026): 11,287.55 (+1.41%)
Recent Performance: April 2026 — led NEPSE rally with +2.97% weekly gain

KEY METRICS FOR MANUFACTURING:
  1. Revenue Growth — Is the company growing its top line?
  2. Operating Margin — Manufacturing margins are thin; above 15% is strong
  3. Capacity Utilization — What % of installed manufacturing capacity is used?
  4. Raw Material Costs — Imports create forex risk (Nepal imports most raw materials)
  5. Debt-to-Equity — Capital-intensive manufacturing often uses debt
  6. Dividend Consistency — Long-established manufacturers often pay regular dividends

VALUATION CONTEXT:
  P/E: 15-25x for established manufacturers
  P/B: 1-3x typical

SECTION B: HOTELS AND TOURISM SECTOR
=============================================================================

Sub-Index: Hotels And Tourism
Listed Companies: 8
Recent Performance: Consistent sector leader in rally sessions
  January 19, 2026: Hotels sector +3.36% (led all sectors)
  January 24, 2026: Hotels sector +3.35% (led all sectors)
  April 10, 2026: Hotels participated in broad rally

KEY METRICS FOR HOTELS:
  1. Occupancy Rate — How full are the hotels? Above 60% is healthy.
  2. Revenue Per Available Room (RevPAR) — Key profitability metric
  3. EBITDA Margin — Hotels have high fixed costs; EBITDA above 20% is good
  4. Tourist Arrivals — Nepal's tourism directly drives hotel revenues
  5. Seasonality — October-November and March-April peak tourist seasons
  6. Debt Load — Most Nepal hotels financed with significant debt
  7. Property Value — Hotel real estate appreciation adds to NAV

VALUATION CONTEXT:
  P/E: 20-50x (varies widely with occupancy cycles)
  P/B: 1-4x (property values factor into book value)
  EV/EBITDA: 10-20x for established properties

Tourism context: Nepal targets 2 million tourists/year. Chinese and Indian tourists
dominate. Trekking and mountain climbing create unique seasonal demand patterns.
Everest Base Camp, Annapurna Circuit, Pashupatinath are key demand drivers.

KEY CATALYSTS FOR HOTELS SECTOR:
  + Tourism recovery post-COVID
  + Government budget announcements supporting tourism (June 2025 budget boosted sector)
  + Nepal Visit Year campaigns
  + Pokhara International Airport (added capacity, new routes)
  - Political instability affects tourist confidence
  - Earthquake risk to infrastructure
  - Competition from Bhutan as premium destination

SECTION D: INVESTMENT SECTOR
=============================================================================

Listed Companies: 7
These are holding companies, mutual fund companies, or investment arms.
Key example: HIDCL (Hydroelectricity Investment & Development Company)
  — Provides financing to hydropower projects
  — Revenue: interest + returns from invested projects
  — Treat as infrastructure investment fund, not direct power company

Other investment companies: Various mutual fund management companies
  Citizen Investment Trust (CIT): Government-backed, large AUM
  NIBL Ace Capital, etc.

VALUATION: Investment companies valued on Net Asset Value (NAV) per unit.
  P/NAV above 1.0 = trading at premium | Below 1.0 = trading at discount.

KEY INSIGHT: Investment sector gains when broader NEPSE rallies because
  their portfolio (equity investments) appreciates. January 19, 2026:
  Investment sector +2.54% (second-best sector that day).

SECTION E: OTHERS / MISCELLANEOUS SECTOR
=============================================================================

Listed Companies: ~10
Includes cable car companies, agritech, cable TV, etc.
Notable: Bandipur Cable Car and Tourism Limited (BCBL) — hit upper circuit Jan 2026.
         Shreenagar Agritech Industries Limited — hit upper circuit Jan 2026.

These stocks are very thinly traded with low liquidity.
High volatility — can hit +10% or -10% circuit breakers on low volume.
Not suitable for fundamental analysis using standard banking metrics.
Use general equity metrics: Revenue, Profit Margin, P/E, P/B.
