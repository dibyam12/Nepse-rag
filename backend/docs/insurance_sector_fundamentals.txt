KEY METRICS FOR INSURANCE STOCKS
=============================================================================

--- LIFE INSURANCE METRICS ---

1. EPS (Earnings Per Share)
   Strong: Above Rs 30 | Moderate: Rs 15-30 | Weak: Below Rs 15

2. Combined Ratio
   Formula: (Claims + Operating Expenses) / Earned Premiums × 100
   Below 100% = profitable underwriting (making money on insurance operations)
   Above 100% = underwriting loss (relying on investment income to be profitable)
   Ideal: 90-98%

3. Solvency Margin
   IAN Regulatory Minimum: 150%
   Must maintain adequate capital to pay future claims
   Higher solvency = more financially secure insurer

4. Embedded Value (EV)
   Present value of future profits + net asset value
   The "true" valuation measure for life insurance companies
   P/EV ratio is how life insurance stocks are valued globally

5. Premium Growth Rate
   YoY growth in gross written premium
   Double-digit premium growth = positive for long-term earnings

6. Investment Income
   Life insurers hold large premium reserves invested in bonds and equities
   A falling stock market directly erodes life insurers' book value
   This is why life insurance stocks are correlated with NEPSE index moves

--- NON-LIFE (GENERAL) INSURANCE METRICS ---

1. EPS — Same interpretation as life insurance
2. Combined Ratio — More important for non-life (short-tail liabilities)
3. Premium Collection — Higher market share = competitive advantage
4. Claims Ratio — Claims paid / Earned premiums × 100. Lower is better.
5. Motor + Property % — Nepal non-life is predominantly motor (vehicle) and property

HOW TO RESPOND TO INSURANCE QUERIES
=============================================================================

When user asks about an insurance stock:

1. Quote LTP, 52W range, market cap
2. State EPS, P/E, and whether P/E is above/below sector average
3. For life insurance: mention investment portfolio sensitivity to NEPSE index
4. For non-life: mention premium market share and combined ratio if available
5. Note IAN regulatory environment — any new capital or solvency requirements
6. Dividend history if available

VALUATION CONTEXT:
  Life insurance stocks typically trade at P/E of 30-80x in Nepal
  Non-life insurance stocks typically trade at P/E of 20-50x
  High P/E in insurance does NOT automatically mean overvalued —
  embedded value, premium growth, and solvency margin matter more.

RISK FACTORS SPECIFIC TO NEPAL INSURANCE:
  - IAN revising solvency margin requirements → capital raising pressure
  - Equity portfolio exposure → life insurer NAV moves with NEPSE index
  - Premium growth slowdown in economic downturns
  - Motor vehicle insurance saturation
  - New entrants after liberalization may compress margins
