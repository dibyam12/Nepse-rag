SECTOR OVERVIEW
=============================================================================

Sector Name: Development Banks
NRB Classification: Class B Banking and Financial Institutions (BFIs)
Total Listed on NEPSE: 10 (as of Q2 FY2082/83)
Role: Bridge between commercial banks and microfinance.
  Serve semi-urban and rural areas, small businesses, agriculture.
Sub-Index: Development Banks
Regulatory Body: Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB)
Minimum Paid-Up Capital: Rs 2.5 Billion (national level)

KEY CHARACTERISTICS vs COMMERCIAL BANKS:
  + Higher Net Interest Margins (NIM): avg 4.4% vs 3.7% for commercial banks
  + More room for operational efficiency improvement
  - Smaller balance sheets and fewer product offerings
  - Higher NPL risk exposure in semi-urban lending
  - Less liquidity in share trading (smaller float)

KEY METRICS TO ANALYZE FOR DEVELOPMENT BANKS
=============================================================================

1. NPL RATIO — Most critical metric. NRB limit: 5%.
   Anything above 5% is a regulatory concern.
   LBBL (0%) and MDB (0.45%) are exceptional. JBBL (7.82%) and EDBL (7.07%) are concerning.

2. NIM (Net Interest Margin) — Development banks should have NIM > 4%.
   Higher NIM = better pricing power and operational efficiency.
   GBBL (4.90%) leads. LBBL (3.98%) is below sector average.

3. ROE — Target: Above 10%.
   GBBL (14%) and MLBL (14.14%) are strongest.
   MDB (6.96%) and LBBL (8.46%) are lower but may be acceptable given low NPL.

4. P/E VALUATION — Sector average P/E is approximately 25-35x.
   P/E below 20x = potential value opportunity.
   MNBBL (17.1x) and GBBL (17.12x) are cheapest on P/E basis.
   JBBL (201.2x) reflects near-zero net earnings — avoid for value investors.

5. PAID-UP CAPITAL — National-level banks require Rs 2.5 Billion minimum.
   Larger capital base generally = more stable, more regulatory compliant.
