SEBON Circulars (Summary)
=========================

This file summarizes key SEBON (Securities Board of Nepal) circulars, directives, and
regulatory themes relevant to NEPSE trading and this system’s vector RAG.

1. Circuit Breaker and Price Band Regulations
---------------------------------------------
- SEBON periodically issues circulars defining:
  - Daily price movement limits (e.g., ±15% from previous close for individual stocks).
  - Index-based circuit breaker thresholds (e.g., intraday trading halts if NEPSE Index
    moves beyond certain percentages within specified time windows).
- Objectives:
  - Protect investors from panic-driven crashes or irrational spikes.
  - Allow time for information digestion during extreme volatility.
- Practical Impact:
  - Trading may halt temporarily or for the rest of the day if index thresholds are hit.
  - Many indicators (RSI, MACD, ATR, Bollinger Bands) behave differently on such days,
    which the system must treat as edge cases.

2. IPO, FPO, and Rights Issue Guidelines
----------------------------------------
- SEBON’s “Securities Registration and Issuance Regulations” and related circulars cover:
  - Eligibility criteria for companies to issue IPOs, FPOs, and right shares.
  - Minimum paid-up capital, profitability, and disclosure requirements.
  - Allotment rules (e.g., minimum kitta per applicant, pro-rata distribution).
  - Timelines for application, allotment, and listing on NEPSE.
- Book Building Process:
  - SEBON has introduced book-building mechanisms for certain large or qualified IPOs.
  - Merchant bankers and issue managers must follow specific pricing, bidding, and
    disclosure procedures.
- Practical Impact:
  - New listings and follow-on offers affect sector supply, index composition, and
    historical price continuity (stock splits, bonuses, rights adjustments).

3. Demat Account and KYC Requirements
-------------------------------------
- SEBON mandates:
  - All investors must hold securities in electronic (dematerialized) form via licensed
    Depository Participants (DPs).
  - Completion of KYC (Know Your Customer) documentation before trading.
- Circular topics include:
  - Standardized KYC forms and periodic KYC renewal.
  - Linking Demat accounts to bank accounts for automatic settlement.
  - Use of electronic platforms (MeroShare) for E-DIS and corporate action requests.
- Practical Impact:
  - Non-compliant or inactive KYC status can block order placement or settlement.
  - Automated settlement flows (T+2) depend on correct Demat-bank linkage.

4. Margin Trading Facility Directives
-------------------------------------
- SEBON’s Margin Trading Facility directives define:
  - Who can provide margin trading (licensed brokers and merchant bankers).
  - Minimum net worth and capital requirements for margin providers.
  - Eligible stocks:
    - Minimum free-float and paid-up capital.
    - Sufficient liquidity and listing history.
    - Positive net worth and profitability criteria.
  - Margin parameters:
    - Initial margin (e.g., at least around 30% of market value).
    - Maintenance margin thresholds and margin-call procedures.
    - Maximum exposure to a single client or related group.
- Practical Impact:
  - Margin-eligible scripts tend to show stronger volume and volatility.
  - Forced selling during margin calls can create sudden spikes in volume and price moves,
    affecting technical indicators and backtests.

5. Broker Commission and Fee Structure
--------------------------------------
- SEBON circulars periodically revise stock broker commission slabs:
  - Slab-based percentage rates depending on transaction size (e.g., different
    rates for trades ≤ 50,000; 50,000–500,000; above 10,000,000, etc.).
  - Caps on maximum brokerage charges per trade.
- Additional Fees:
  - SEBON regulatory fee and NEPSE transaction fee.
  - Clearing and settlement charges.
  - DP charges for Demat and settlement.
- Practical Impact:
  - Net profitability of strategies must account for total transaction costs, not
    just raw price changes.
  - High commission burden can erode the edge of high-frequency or low-margin strategies.

6. Foreign Investment and Repatriation Rules
--------------------------------------------
- SEBON coordinates with the Department of Industry and Nepal Rastra Bank to regulate:
  - Foreign portfolio investment in listed companies (NEPSE scripts).
  - Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) thresholds and sector-specific restrictions.
  - Requirements for approval, registration, and reporting of foreign inflows.
  - Rules for repatriation of dividends and capital gains in foreign currency.
- Practical Impact:
  - Changes in foreign investment limits or eligibility can alter demand for certain
    sectors (e.g., banks, hydropower), impacting long-term trend behavior.
  - Large foreign inflows or outflows may cause unusual volume spikes and trend shifts.

7. Disclosure, Corporate Governance, and Insider Trading
-------------------------------------------------------
- SEBON’s circulars and codes of conduct address:
  - Timely disclosure of price-sensitive information (bonuses, rights, mergers,
    major investments, board changes).
  - Corporate governance standards and reporting obligations of listed companies.
  - Prohibition and penalties for insider trading, market manipulation, and
    circular trading.
- Practical Impact:
  - Delayed or sudden disclosures can cause gaps in price and volume, which indicators
    may misinterpret as normal technical signals.
  - News-driven moves should be tagged as such in backtests wherever possible.

8. Mutual Funds, Debentures, and Other Instruments
--------------------------------------------------
- SEBON issues specific guidelines for:
  - Mutual funds and collective investment schemes (CIS).
  - Debentures, preference shares, and other fixed-income or hybrid instruments.
  - Listing, trading, and disclosure norms for these instruments.
- Practical Impact:
  - Different instruments exhibit different volatility and liquidity profiles; the
    system should not apply equity-oriented indicator thresholds blindly to all
    instruments.
  - Sector and instrument mix on NEPSE affects overall index behavior.

9. T+2 Settlement and Risk Management Circulars
-----------------------------------------------
- SEBON and NEPSE coordinate rules on:
  - Transition and adherence to T+2 settlement.
  - Risk management framework for brokers, clearing members, and CDSC.
  - Use of collateral, default funds, and penalty structures.
- Practical Impact:
  - Settlement failures can lead to close-out trades and abnormal price/volume bars.
  - Such bars must be treated as anomalies or flagged as special events during
    model training.

10. 24-Hour Order Placement and System Reforms
----------------------------------------------
- Recent SEBON and NEPSE reforms introduce:
  - The ability to place orders round the clock (outside trading hours), which will
    be queued and executed during market sessions.
  - Updated circuit breaker rules, improved risk controls, and technology standards
    for brokers’ trading systems.
- Practical Impact:
  - Order flow becomes more continuous, though execution remains restricted to
    trading hours.
  - Pre-market and post-market order buildup can affect opening gaps and early-session
    volatility, which RSI, MACD, and other indicators must account for during
    backtesting.
