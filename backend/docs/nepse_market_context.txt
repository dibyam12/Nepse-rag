NEPSE MARKET STRUCTURE
=============================================================================

Full Name: Nepal Stock Exchange Limited (NEPSE)
Founded: January 13, 1994
Type: Government-owned stock exchange (only one in Nepal)
Location: Singhadurbar, Kathmandu, Nepal
Regulator: SEBON (Securities Board of Nepal)
Clearing: CDS and Clearing Limited (CDSC)

TRADING BASICS:
  Trading Days: Sunday to Thursday (Nepal's working week)
  Trading Hours: 11:00 AM — 3:00 PM NPT (Nepal Time, UTC+5:45)
  Pre-Open Session: 10:45-11:00 AM
  Settlement: T+2 (two working days after trade)
  Trading Platform: NEPSE Online Trading System (NOTS)
  Face Value: Rs 100 per share (most companies)
  Lot Size: 10 shares minimum for most stocks; varies by category

PRICE LIMITS (Circuit Breakers):
  Daily Upper Circuit: +10% above previous close
  Daily Lower Circuit: -10% below previous close
  Exception: On listing day (IPO first trade), maximum gain is 10%
  Index-level circuit: Trading halts if index falls 5% intraday

MARKET INDICES:
  NEPSE Index: Benchmark composite index
  NEPSE Sensitive Index: Tracks only Class A stocks (commercial banks, large caps)
  NEPSE Float Index: Tracks only publicly floating shares

=============================================================================

Currency: Nepalese Rupee (NPR)
  Exchange Rate: NPR is PEGGED to INR at 1 INR = 1.60 NPR (fixed by NRB)
  Implication: Nepal monetary policy closely follows India's RBI decisions

NRB (Nepal Rastra Bank) — CURRENT MONETARY POLICY:
  Repo Rate (Bank Rate): 4.25%
  NRB Bank Rate: 5.75%
  Refinancing Rate (for productive sectors): 5.00-6.50%
  Average Fixed Deposit Rate (commercial banks): 5.18%
  Average Lending Rate (commercial banks): 7.00%

REMITTANCE ECONOMY:
  Nepal is one of the world's most remittance-dependent economies.
  Remittances ~25-30% of GDP.
  Impact on NEPSE: Remittance inflows → bank deposits → credit growth → stock market liquidity.
  Strong remittances = positive for banking stocks and NEPSE overall.

KEY SEBON REGULATIONS INVESTORS MUST KNOW
=============================================================================

1. BONUS SHARE AND DIVIDENDS:
   Companies announce cash dividend and/or bonus shares at AGM.
   Book closure date: Shareholders registered before this date receive dividend.
   Ex-dividend date: Stock price typically falls by dividend amount on this date.
   Cash dividend: Paid in cash. Taxed at 5% at source (for individuals).
   Bonus share: New shares issued at Rs 100 face value. Tax treatment differs.

2. RIGHTS OFFERING:
   Companies can issue new shares to existing shareholders at a discount.
   Rights ratio: e.g., 1:3 means 1 new share for every 3 existing shares held.
   Subscription price: Usually Rs 100-200 (face value or small premium).
   If you DON'T subscribe: Your stake gets diluted.
   Rights shares can be RENOUNCED (sold in secondary market during subscription period).

3. FPO (Further Public Offering):
   Public offering after IPO. Often by established companies to raise more capital.
   Regulated by SEBON. Prospectus required.

4. MARGIN LENDING:
   Investors can borrow against shares for additional buying.
   NRB regulates margin lending — banks can lend up to 65% of share value.
   Margin calls during market corrections can amplify selloffs.

5. FOREIGN INVESTMENT IN NEPSE:
   Foreign portfolio investment was restricted for most of NEPSE's history.
   Recent changes allow NRNs (Non-Resident Nepalis) and some foreign investors.
   Institutional foreign investment remains limited.

6. INSIDER TRADING:
   SEBON has insider trading regulations but enforcement is limited.
   Board member and promoter trading is regulated and must be disclosed.

7. PROMOTER SHARE LOCK-IN:
   Promoter shares are locked in for 3-5 years after IPO.
   Large promoter lock-in expiry = potential selling pressure (watch for this).

=============================================================================
NEPSE TRADING CONCEPTS
=============================================================================

FLOOR PRICE:
  SEBON can set a floor (minimum) price for IPOs and rights shares.
  Floor price = face value (Rs 100) + premium if applicable.

OVERSUBSCRIPTION:
  When demand for IPO/rights shares exceeds supply.
  Allotment done by lottery (balloting) for retail investors.
  100x-500x oversubscription is common for popular hydropower IPOs.

QUALIFIED INSTITUTIONAL BUYERS (QIB):
  Separate allotment quota for institutions (mutual funds, banks, etc.).

MUTUAL FUNDS:
  Nepal has growing mutual fund industry.
  Close-ended funds listed on NEPSE (trade like stocks).
  Open-ended funds: NAV-based pricing, not NEPSE-listed.
  Nabil Equity Fund, Siddhartha Equity Scheme, etc.

DEMAT ACCOUNT:
  All shares held in dematerialized form through CDS (Central Depository System).
  Mandatory for trading. Linked to bank account.
  MEROSHARE: CDS's online platform for IPO applications, portfolio viewing.

BROKER SYSTEM:
  Trading through licensed brokers only.
  Online trading available through NOTS (NEPSE Online Trading System).
  Broker commission: 0.40% for transactions below Rs 50,000;
  reduces on higher transaction amounts.
  DP fee (depository participant): Rs 25 per buy/sell transaction.
