=============================================================================
SECTOR OVERVIEW
=============================================================================

Sector Name: Microfinance
NRB Classification: Class D BFIs (Laghubitta Bittiya Sanstha)
Total Listed on NEPSE: ~50 companies (down from 55 in 2023 due to mergers)
Regulatory Body: Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB)
Mission: Financial inclusion — provide credit to underserved rural, semi-urban women.
Minimum Paid-Up Capital: Rs 200 Million (national level)

WHY MICROFINANCE STOCKS TRADE AT PREMIUM:
  - Historically very HIGH EPS (Rs 40-100+) due to small equity base
  - High ROE (15-30%) due to efficient capital deployment
  - Government priority sector — subsidized refinancing from NRB
  - Group lending model = lower default risk historically
  - Target clients: rural women, small farmers (politically protected)

CURRENT SECTOR CHALLENGES (2024-2026):
  - Loan recovery issues in post-COVID economy
  - Rising NPL / PAR (Portfolio at Risk) across sector
  - NRB tightening capital requirements → forced mergers
  - Industry-wide profit DECLINED 42.58% in recent periods
  - Sector consolidation: 65 companies → ~50 through M&A
  - Interest income under pressure from NRB rate caps on microfinance loans

=============================================================================
KEY METRICS FOR MICROFINANCE STOCKS
=============================================================================

1. EPS (Earnings Per Share)
   Strong: Above Rs 40 | Moderate: Rs 20-40 | Weak: Below Rs 20
   Note: Small equity base inflates EPS — always check PAR alongside EPS.

2. PAR (Portfolio at Risk) / NPL Ratio
   Formula: Loans overdue > 30 days ÷ Total Gross Loan Portfolio × 100
   Critical indicator for microfinance.
   Excellent: Below 3% | Acceptable: 3-5% | Concerning: Above 5% | Danger: Above 10%
   Sector PAR increased significantly in 2023-2024 period.

3. GLP (Gross Loan Portfolio)
   Total amount of active loans outstanding.
   Growing GLP with controlled PAR = healthy expansion.
   Growing GLP with rising PAR = credit quality deterioration.

4. ROE (Return on Equity)
   Strong: Above 15% | Moderate: 10-15% | Weak: Below 10%

5. Cost of Funds
   Interest paid to depositors and borrowers of wholesale funds (NRB refinancing).
   Lower cost of funds = better net interest margin.
   NRB provides refinancing at subsidized rates to microfinance companies.

6. Operating Efficiency Ratio
   Operating costs ÷ Gross Income × 100.
   Lower is better. Rural outreach increases costs.

7. CAPITAL ADEQUACY RATIO (CAR)
   NRB Minimum: 8% for microfinance
   Adequate buffer above minimum = safer institution

8. NUMBER OF ACTIVE BORROWERS
   Scale measure. More borrowers = better diversification.
   Also indicates social impact.

9. FEMALE BORROWER %
   Most national-level microfinance target 95%+ female borrowers.
   NRB regulatory requirement for classification as microfinance BFI.

SECTOR PROFIT CONTEXT (Recent Trend)
=============================================================================

DECLINING PROFITS (2022-2024 period):
  - Net Profit of Microfinance Sector DECLINED 42.58% year-over-year in 2022.
  - Driven by rising provisioning requirements for bad loans.
  - CBBL: Highest net profit (Rs 55.61 Crore, national category)
  - JBLB: Second highest (Rs 26.47 Crore, national category)
  - SKBBL: Highest profit at national-wholesale level

RECOVERY INDICATORS (2025-2026):
  - NRB providing refinancing at low rates to support sector
  - Forced mergers reducing overcrowding
  - Stronger institutions absorbing weaker ones
  - Regulatory framework becoming clearer

=============================================================================
HOW TO RESPOND TO MICROFINANCE QUERIES
=============================================================================

When user asks about a microfinance stock:

1. Identify category: National level vs regional/district level
   (National level = higher quality, more regulatory oversight)

2. State EPS context: Microfinance EPS of Rs 30+ is strong.
   But ALWAYS check PAR/NPL alongside EPS.

3. Mention sector-wide challenge: Loan recovery issues and declining profits
   have affected the entire sector from 2022-2024. Check if the specific company
   has recovered.

4. P/E for microfinance: Typically 20-40x for quality companies.
   P/E above 50x = expensive unless EPS growth justifies it.

5. Note ongoing NRB-driven consolidation — mergers can create short-term
   disruption but long-term strength for the surviving entity.

6. Mention group lending model: Peer accountability reduces default risk
   compared to individual loans in banks.

IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER FOR MICROFINANCE:
  Historical EPS data for microfinance (before FY2082) may be significantly
  different from current EPS due to:
  - Rising NPL and provisioning requirements
  - Mandatory capital increases
  - Post-merger integration adjustments
  Always use the most recent quarterly data available.
