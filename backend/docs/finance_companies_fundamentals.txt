SECTOR OVERVIEW
=============================================================================

Sector Name: Finance Companies
NRB Classification: Class C Banking and Financial Institutions (BFIs)
Total Listed on NEPSE: 10 (as of Q2 FY2082/83)
Role: Consumer loans, hire purchase financing, leasing, small business loans.
  Operate in urban/semi-urban markets with narrow product focus.
Regulatory Body: Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB)
Minimum Paid-Up Capital: Rs 1 Billion

KEY CHARACTERISTICS:
  + Highest NIM in the BFI space: avg ~6% (vs 4.4% dev banks, 3.7% comm banks)
  + Some companies have extraordinarily high EPS due to small equity base
  - MUCH higher NPL risk: sector avg NPL ~8-12% (vs 3.6% for commercial banks)
  - Smallest balance sheets in the BFI hierarchy
  - Less diversified — concentrated in consumer/hire-purchase lending
  - Some companies showing signs of distress (GUFL, PFL NPL risk)

IMPORTANT WARNING: High EPS in finance companies can be MISLEADING.
A small equity base inflates both EPS and ROE. Always check NPL ratio alongside EPS.
PFL's EPS of Rs 43.20 is the highest in all BFI but its NPL of 25.1% is existential risk.

HOW TO ANALYZE FINANCE COMPANIES
=============================================================================

Step 1 — Always check NPL FIRST. If NPL > 10%, treat EPS as potentially inflated.
Step 2 — Check NAV. Low NAV (below Rs 100) with high EPS = small equity base effect.
Step 3 — Check NIM. Finance companies with NIM > 6% have strong pricing power.
Step 4 — Compare ROE excluding PFL (which is an outlier at 65.36%).
         Sector avg ROE excluding PFL: ~5.05% — much lower than it appears.
Step 5 — P/E context: Finance company P/E of 30-40x is reasonable for quality companies.
         P/E above 50x needs justification. Negative P/E = actual losses likely.
